Founded in Chicago in 1892

Aronberg Goldgehn Davis & Garmisa was founded nearly 120 years ago in Chicago when two young attorneys started their own law firm, Newman & Northrup. The firm focused on handling business transactions and banking matters, and during the Great Depression became a strategic corporate advisor to many of Chicago’s large corporations, such as Goodyear and Booth Fisheries.

After the trying years of World War I, one of the firm’s partners won the Bok Peace Prize for assisting in the drafting of the Kellogg-Briand Peace Pact, which is still considered a major milestone in efforts to advance international peace. During World War II, several members of the firm answered a call to duty and nine partners gave their lives for their country.

A Full-Service Mid-Sized Firm

With vision, steadfast determination and a continuing commitment to a high level of quality and integrity, the firm navigated the many obstacles posed by an increasingly complex, volatile and ever-changing business environment. Aronberg Goldgehn managed these challenges and grew to become a successful mid-sized law firm able to effectively provide legal representation to businesses, families and individuals in nearly every major area of practice.
